Modern MP Office Operations

Civic work needs memory, evidence, and movement.

Citizen issues, scheme updates, field reports, public consultations, and parliamentary questions arrive with incomplete context. The office needs a way to preserve facts, identify jurisdiction, assign follow-up, and keep learning across every case.

View the demo Open evidence board

The Operating Gap

One civic issue can arrive as a hundred separate complaints.

A pothole in Bengaluru may be reported through typed notes, voice inputs, photos, different languages, and slightly different locations. A form-based system can classify these inputs into the same broad category, but still treat them as separate complaints. A public office needs AI that can identify the shared issue, preserve the evidence, route it to the right authority, and update every affected citizen when progress is made.

01

Inputs arrive in many forms

Citizens may describe the same issue through free text, voice, photos, location pins, ward names, or partial landmarks.

02

Duplicates hide the real issue

A hundred reports about one pothole can become a hundred records unless language, image and location signals are clustered.

03

Ownership determines movement

The office must know whether the next step sits with BBMP, ward engineers, police, state agencies, central schemes, or elected representatives.

04

Institutions need memory

Every resolved case should improve future triage through reusable evidence packs, escalation paths, response templates, and closure criteria.

The Product Idea

A co-pilot for evidence-backed public leadership operations.

The prototype simulates how an MP office can confirm a drafted civic input and update the workstreams required for responsible action: jurisdiction, evidence, stakeholders, public communication, parliamentary research, privacy, and institutional memory.

Convert inputs into cases

Messy citizen notes, voice inputs and photos become structured issue clusters with facts, evidence, ownership and next questions.

Protect action discipline

The system separates what the office can do, what must be escalated, what needs public consultation, and what requires human approval.

Build institutional memory

Each case leaves behind templates, evidence standards, closure patterns, and reusable knowledge for future staff and volunteers.

Defined Demo Use Case

Bengaluru civic infrastructure issue enters the office

The demo follows a public input about a road infrastructure proposal, missing consultation records, traffic concerns, and unclear authority. The co-pilot helps the office convert that input into an evidence-backed route for review, escalation, communication, and institutional learning.

What the demo shows

  • Citizen input converted into a structured civic case.
  • Jurisdiction mapped across office, BBMP, state, and central touchpoints.
  • Evidence gaps identified before public communication.
  • Follow-up and closure criteria made explicit.
  • Institutional memory created for future civic issues.
